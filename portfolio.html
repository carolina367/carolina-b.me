** Third Place App **

The concept of the "third place," as coined by Ray Oldenburg in The Great Goodplace, refers to social environments distinct from the conventional settings of home and the workplace. These spaces play a crucial role in fostering community, social interaction, and a sense of belonging. Examples include cafes, bars, community centers, libraries, and other locales where people gather beyond their homes and workplaces.

In essence, the third place serves as a vital component for a well-rounded and fulfilling life, complementing the private sphere of home and the professional realm of work. It is characterized by inclusivity and sociability, providing individuals with opportunities for casual encounters, discussions, and shared experiences.

I would like to create an extension to google maps that can identify these spaces and describe their atmosphere. I particularly would want it to have indicators of if there are paywalls to be there. I often find it hard when I'm in a new city to find good places to study since there is not one word that encompasses all the different types of study vibes. This is a planned pursuit.

** Automated Blinds **

Transforming my blinds into smart, motorized systems involves selecting blinds suitable for motorization, like roller or corded blinds. After choosing a DC motor that matches the blinds' size and weight, I will integrate it with a motor controller. Power will be supplied through a battery or plug-in source, and I'll opt for a convenient control system—remote, app, or smart home integration. Installation includes attaching the motor, concealing wires for a tidy look, and ensuring safety. Thorough testing guarantees seamless response to control commands. Optionally, sensors for automated adjustments based on environmental conditions are considered. The final touch involves securing the blinds and motor, with compatibility for integration into existing smart home setups.

Battleship

My Battleship game was crafted using Java with a JavaFX GUI. The game mirrors the strategic essence of the classic Battleship, challenging players to locate their adversary’s fleet on a grid. The GUI, designed for clarity and ease of use, provides a visually appealing interface. Single-player mode is available, pitting users against a computer opponent powered by a strategic algorithm. The codebase is structured and commented for potential future enhancements. This project serves as a testament to proficiency in Java and JavaFX, demonstrating the ability to create an interactive and engaging digital version of a classic game.
